The Cute Word

You were unpacking a new life,

new streets,

new skies,

new reasons to stay up at night.

And I was unpacking old pain,

old doubts,

old wounds,

old storms that had learned to hide in plain sight.

I waited too long.

That's the truth.

Long enough for hurt to grow roots.

Long enough for silence to turn into weight,

and weight into something I could no longer contain.

So when the box finally opened,

it wasn't grace that fell out.

It was anger.

It was sorrow.

It was every conversation

I should have had yesterday,

arriving all at once tomorrow.

You told me,

"Harsh is a cute word"

And God,

I think about that now.

Because I heard the words then,

but I hear the ache now.

I needed you to feel

what I had carried alone.

But somewhere along the road,

I forgot that you were carrying things of your own.

So if my words left bruises,

if they echoed after the fight was done,

if they made you feel less loved

instead of more understood,

then I am sorry.

Not for speaking.

Not for finally letting the truth be known.

But for turning my pain into a stone

and placing it in your hands

just to prove how heavy it had grown.

So I'm sorry for the wounds, even if the reasons were real.

I'm sorry for making you bleed while asking you to see how I feel.
